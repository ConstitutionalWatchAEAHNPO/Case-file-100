III. Evidence of Tortious Conduct

This record documents a pattern of "Predatory Security Management." Despite having explicit knowledge that the tenant is currently domiciled in Pennsylvania and has satisfied all financial arrears—including secondary fees—the facility has willfully failed to re-secure the unit after forcibly removing the tenant's security measures.

"The facility agent (Subject) initiated contact knowing the tenant was out-of-state. Upon payment of the lien, the facility failed to return the unit to its prior secured state. Under Minnesota Statutes § 514.972 (Self-Service Storage; Lien), the owner may only act in a 'commercially reasonable manner.' Maintaining an unsecured unit containing a tenant’s total residential property after payment is Prima Facie evidence of Gross Negligence and Breach of the Covenant of Good Faith and Fair Dealing."
IV. Statutory Violations Called Out
  1. MN Stat. § 514.973 (Enforcement of Lien): Management utilized the threat of sale to extract payment but failed to follow the reciprocal duty of care to protect the collateral once the debt was satisfied.
  2. MN Stat. § 325F.69 (Consumer Fraud Act): Misleading the consumer into believing that paying a fee "three times larger" than the base rent would result in the continued protection of their property.
  3. Constructive Bailment: By cutting the lock, the facility assumed physical control of the assets. Their refusal to re-lock the gate constitutes an intentional abandonment of the tenant's property.
V. Grounds for Litigation (The Suit)

The tenant (Plaintiff) intends to pursue a civil action against Public Storage on the following grounds:

  • Intentional Infliction of Emotional Distress (IIED): Leveraging the distance of the out-of-state tenant to create high-stress scenarios involving the potential loss of all worldly possessions.
  • Conversion (Threatened): Utilizing lien statutes as a weapon to extract excessive fees without providing the fundamental service of "storage" (which requires security).
  • Breach of Contract: Failure to provide a secure environment as guaranteed by the rental agreement.
